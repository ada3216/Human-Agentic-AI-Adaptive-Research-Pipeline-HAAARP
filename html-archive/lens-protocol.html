Researcher Lens Protocol

Structured reflexivity dialogue for human–AI collaborative qualitative analysis
Version: Option B — Two-Pass Design Strands: IPA + Psychodynamic Discourse Analysis Epistemology: Constructivist–Interpretivist

Two-Pass Analysis Flow

Pass 1 — Blind
AI reads all data
no researcher lens
→
Lens Conversation
structured reflexivity
dialogue with AI
→
Pass 2 — Positioned
AI re-analyses through
researcher lens
→
Human Synthesis
interpretive top layer
+ triangulation
The lens conversation occurs between Pass 1 and Pass 2. It externalises the researcher's positionality into an explicit, documented interpretive frame — transforming a potential source of bias into a methodologically accounted instrument. The gap between Pass 1 and Pass 2 outputs is itself analytically generative.
01

Before the Lens Conversation — Setup

both strands

Complete Pass 1 before any part of this protocol. The AI should have read and produced first-pass annotations across all interview transcripts (IPA strand) and all session transcripts (PDA strand) without any researcher framing. Only then begin this conversation.

Instruction to AI — opening the lens conversation

Tell the AI: "You have now read all the data. I want to have a structured conversation about my own perspective, theoretical background, and assumptions before we do a second interpretive pass. I will answer a series of questions. Your job is to listen, ask follow-up questions where something is unclear, and then summarise back to me a 'researcher lens document' that we will use to frame the second analysis. Do not begin any analysis yet."

Methodological note Having the AI summarise back what it heard before Pass 2 begins creates a written audit trail. This becomes part of your reflexivity appendix and demonstrates rigour in your methodology chapter.
02

Theoretical & Clinical Positioning

both strands

These questions establish your theoretical home — the conceptual language through which you naturally make sense of therapeutic encounters. This becomes the vocabulary the AI will use when framing interpretive observations in Pass 2.

How would you describe the integrative model the therapist is using, in your own words?
What are the core theoretical traditions being drawn on? How do they sit together in this therapist's practice? What does 'integrative' mean here specifically — is it theoretically integrative, technically eclectic, or something else?
establishes the primary theoretical lens for reading session transcripts in the PDA strand
What relational or psychodynamic concepts feel most alive and relevant to this research for you?
For example: transference, countertransference, attachment, rupture and repair, the therapeutic alliance, projective identification, intersubjectivity. Which of these do you expect to appear, and in what form?
identifies the psychodynamic vocabulary to be sensitive to in both strands
What does 'existential experience' mean to you in the context of therapy?
Are you thinking about this in terms of meaning-making, mortality salience, isolation, freedom, identity — or something else? How do you expect it to show up in either what participants say in interview, or what happens in the sessions?
anchors the existential dimension of the research question so the AI can flag relevant material
03

Researcher Positionality & Reflexivity

core lens

This is the heart of the lens conversation. In constructivist qualitative research, the researcher is not a neutral instrument. These questions make your positionality explicit so it can be held consciously rather than operating as invisible background noise.

What is your relationship to this topic — personally, clinically, or professionally?
How did you come to this research question? What draws you to it? Is there anything in your own clinical or personal experience that connects to what you are studying?
surfaces the researcher's emotional and experiential investment in the topic
Do you hold any particular assumptions about what it is like to work with a therapist who is perceived as 'different'?
What do you think clients might feel? What challenges or opportunities do you expect this creates in the therapeutic relationship? Try to be as honest as possible — these are not wrong answers, they are data about your lens.
makes implicit hypotheses explicit so the AI can both work with them and flag material that challenges them
What would it mean for this research if participants reported largely positive experiences? What if they reported largely difficult ones?
Would either outcome surprise you? What do you find yourself hoping for, if anything? What would feel uncomfortable or challenging to find?
reveals the researcher's stake in the findings — important for disconfirmation instruction in Pass 2
Is there anything about your own identity, background, or experience that positions you in a particular way in relation to this research?
You do not need to disclose anything you are not comfortable with. But anything you are willing to name helps the AI understand the lens through which you are approaching interpretation.
researcher discretion applies — share only what you are comfortable documenting in a methodology appendix
04

Hypothesis Mapping — What You Expect to Find

core lens

Making your expectations explicit before Pass 2 enables the AI to both orient toward them and actively monitor for material that resists them. This is not an invitation to confirm — it is an invitation to test.

If you had to guess: what do you think the IPA interviews will reveal about how participants experienced working with this therapist?
What themes do you expect to emerge — in terms of the lived experience of the therapeutic relationship, the existential dimensions, the relational dynamics they describe?
generates candidate IPA themes to test against the data in Pass 2
What do you expect to see in the session transcripts at the discursive level?
Where do you anticipate moments of relational intensity — rupture, repair, avoidance, displacement? What linguistic or interactional features do you expect to carry psychodynamic weight in these transcripts?
generates candidate PDA foci for Pass 2 — moments to look for and around
What do you think you might have already noticed in Pass 1 that surprised you?
Having seen the first-pass AI output — was there anything that felt unexpected, that didn't fit your expectations, that seemed interesting or strange? What in the data is pulling at you?
integrates what the blind pass surfaced into the lens before Pass 2 — keeps the researcher genuinely responsive to the data
05

Disconfirmation Instructions

pass 2 framing

This section is given to the AI as an explicit instruction before Pass 2 begins. It is the methodological guard against over-fitting to the researcher lens.

Instruction to AI — to be given before Pass 2

Tell the AI: "You now have my researcher lens. When you do the second pass, I want you to do two things simultaneously: first, read the data through this lens and flag material that is illuminated by it. Second, and equally importantly, actively look for material that does not fit — moments that resist the lens, that sit outside my expectations, that complicate or contradict what I told you I expected to find. Mark these clearly. The gap between what the lens predicts and what the data actually does is where the most important findings may live."

AI should surface

  • Material that fits the lens — with confidence rating
  • Material that partially fits — with note on what diverges
  • Material that actively resists the lens
  • Patterns not anticipated in the lens conversation
  • Moments where the two strands diverge (what participant said vs what happened in session)

Guard against

  • Only surfacing confirming material
  • Reframing disconfirming data to fit the lens
  • Treating the lens as a coding scheme rather than an interpretive frame
  • Flattening ambiguity in the data to resolve it
06

Lens Summary — AI Output Template

audit trail

After the lens conversation, ask the AI to produce this document before Pass 2 begins. This becomes your reflexivity appendix.

Based on our conversation, please produce a Researcher Lens Summary with the following sections: 1. Theoretical framework [AI summarises the integrative model and psychodynamic concepts named] 2. Researcher positionality [AI summarises stated personal/clinical relationship to the topic] 3. Explicit hypotheses going into Pass 2 [IPA strand: expected themes / PDA strand: expected discursive features] 4. Concepts to be sensitive to [vocabulary, constructs, relational dynamics named by researcher] 5. Disconfirmation mandate [restate the explicit instruction to flag non-fitting material] 6. Anything the researcher flagged from Pass 1 as surprising [integrating blind-pass observations into the lens] Confirm back to me if this accurately represents your understanding before we proceed to Pass 2.
Documentation note Save this lens summary document. It forms part of your methodology audit trail and should be referenced in your reflexivity section. It demonstrates that AI use in the analysis was structured, transparent, and epistemologically grounded — not a black box.

07

Methodological Framing for Write-Up

methodology chapter

How to describe this process in your methodology chapter — language you can adapt.

Suggested framing

"A structured reflexivity protocol was employed between the first and second AI-assisted analytic passes. Following an initial blind pass of all data — in which no researcher framing was provided to the model — the researcher engaged in a structured dialogue with the AI system to articulate their theoretical commitments, clinical positionality, and a priori hypotheses. The AI produced a researcher lens summary which was confirmed prior to the second pass. This process externalised the reflexivity practice required by constructivist IPA methodology, creating a documented audit trail of the researcher's interpretive position. The AI was explicitly instructed to surface disconfirming material alongside lens-consistent findings, and the gap between blind and positioned passes was treated as analytically generative."
